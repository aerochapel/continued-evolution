The Real Test

03 Apr 2025

The real test isn’t whether people notice your work. It’s whether you keep creating, building, showing up—when no one’s watching, reading, or listening. Not out of bitterness. Not out of performance. But because something inside you knows it’s the right thing to do.

Zydrunas Savickas, one of the strongest men to ever live, trains alone. Not for isolation’s sake, but because it forces him to grow without relying on energy from the crowd. He says if he can lift without that external boost, then when the competition comes and the audience roars, it will add to what’s already there. The crowd becomes a supplement—not a crutch. That’s true power.

The same is true of a great film. Take away the score, the sound, the emotional scaffolding—and if the scene still holds your attention in silence, you know you’re watching something real. Most things rely on noise. The best things don’t need it.

And it’s the same with people. There’s a quiet strength in those who train without music, who write without readers, who design, build, serve, think, reflect—without applause. When life is silent and unrewarding, and you still give your time, your attention, your creativity, that’s the real test of character.

Sometimes that shows up as someone making art no one sees. Sometimes it’s someone taking care of their mother every single day without recognition. Sometimes it’s a person choosing the long, quiet path of integrity over shortcuts and spectacle.

There’s a quiet power in people who don’t need to be seen to do good work. Because their effort is not performative—it’s integrated. All of their experiences, instincts, values, and perspectives coalesce into one unwavering force. They don’t need noise. They don’t need permission. They already know it matters.

The truth is always revealed in what we do when no one’s looking.

“But when you give to the needy, do not let your left hand know what your right hand is doing, so that your giving may be in secret. Then your Father, who sees what is done in secret, will reward you.”
—Matthew 6:3–4

I believe the future will be led by the quiet constructors—those who have been toiling away in silence, without the need for validation from social media or the spotlight. And when all of that noise becomes irrelevant—when the platforms fade and the applause dims—it will be those who trained in solitude who rise. While the ones who depended on attention for their relevance will quietly disappear into the background.

That’s the real test.
